Heritage Sites: Journeys Through Time

Step back in time and explore the historical landmarks that tell the story of Lucena and Quezon Province, reflecting their rich past.

Saint Ferdinand Cathedral

A significant religious landmark in Lucena City, showcasing beautiful Spanish colonial architecture and a rich spiritual history dating back to 1881.

See in Waze

Quezon Provincial Capitol

An impressive architectural blend that serves as the seat of the provincial government. Its grandeur reflects the province's historical and political significance.

See in Waze

Puente de Malagonlong (Tayabas)

Just a short drive away in Tayabas, this 19th-century Spanish colonial bridge, built in 1840, is the longest and oldest in Quezon Province. A true engineering marvel of its time.

See in Waze

Kamay Ni Hesus Healing Church (Lucban)

A popular pilgrimage site featuring a towering statue of the Ascending Christ atop a 300-step grotto, offering stunning views and spiritual reflection.

See in Waze

Tayabas Cemetery Chapel

A historical site in Tayabas, this chapel is an example of intricate stone architecture from the Spanish colonial period, reflecting a unique heritage.

See in Waze

Minor Basilica of St. Michael the Archangel (Tayabas)

Recognized as a National Cultural Treasure, this majestic church in Tayabas features unique characteristics and serves as a testament to religious art and architecture.
